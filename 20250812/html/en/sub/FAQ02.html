FAQ

All the answers to your questions are here.

From equipment to solutions, we've compiled the most frequently asked questions from customers.

NEXUSGAS supplies hydrogen equipment products that have obtained KGS, KC, and explosion-proof certifications, and installs products with certified specifications according to project requirements.
We basically have industrial LPG storage tanks in units of 0.5 tons, 1 ton, 2 tons, and 5 tons, and customization such as horizontal/vertical, above-ground/buried types is also possible according to customer requirements. The tank exterior is finished with special paint to prevent corrosion, and we provide selection guides according to installation environment.
It usually takes 2-4 weeks depending on the production schedule, and in case of urgent delivery, early shipment or intermediate finished product delivery is also possible after prior consultation.
We basically use domestically certified high-pressure valves, and for special specifications or high/low temperature valves, imported valves from the US Cryostar, Germany WITT, Japan Kitz, etc. can also be applied.
Hydrogen charging station facilities require high-pressure gas handling permits and special facility review procedures for use equipment, and NEXUSGAS provides construction-centered Turn-Key services (including permit and inspection linkage).
High-pressure gas equipment and storage containers must be installed by qualified professional construction companies. If general customers install them arbitrarily, they may be subject to legal punishment, and there is a risk of equipment damage and explosion. We provide related evidence such as technical qualification certificates and work logs before construction, and construct under on-site responsibility.
The basic free warranty is 1 year from the shipping date, and can be extended up to 2 years depending on product type and contract conditions.
They are provided in PDF format on the website [Resources] menu or each [Product Introduction] detail page. Technical specification sheets for each item are also provided, and if English versions and CAD drawings are needed, they will be delivered separately upon consultation request. Offline printed copies can be delivered upon request.
Yes. NEXUSGAS supplies valves, measuring instruments, cable glands, etc. with certifications for each explosion-proof rating such as Ex d, Ex e, Ex n, and has equipment lineups applicable to hazardous material storage and Zone 1/2 areas. For explosion-proof equipment, certificate copies and manufacturer warranties are also provided together during installation.

Construction period varies depending on site conditions, equipment type, and process scale. Generally, for medium-sized hydrogen piping systems, construction can be completed within 60-90 days after design approval, and LPG storage facility installation can be completed within 30 days. Since it varies depending on whether surveying, drawing finalization, and permit schedules are included, we provide individual proposals for schedules after site inspection.
NEXUSGAS performs the entire construction process one-stop according to specifications agreed with customers after design completion, and can achieve both schedule reduction and cost efficiency through our own material procurement and construction.
While not legally mandatory, regular inspections are recommended according to Article 33 of the High Pressure Gas Safety Management Act, and annual maintenance contracts are recommended for stable facility operation. Contracts include regular visit inspections, failure response, and abnormality alert response.
According to maintenance contracts, we basically conduct regular inspections twice a year (first and second half), and for high-pressure facilities or public institution facilities, quarterly inspections (4 times a year) can be expanded. Inspection items include leak tests, valve condition checks, instrument calibration, and pipe fixing condition checks.
Yes. Construction is possible in hazardous material handling areas, explosion-proof areas, and high-pressure gas zones within industrial complexes, and we construct based on industrial safety and health plans, hot work plans, and explosion-proof certified products. When necessary, we also write safety documents such as job safety analysis (JSA) and council meeting minutes.
Yes, it is possible. For production facility areas where process shutdown is difficult or emergency construction, night or holiday construction personnel deployment is possible, and in this case, prior work plan consultation and separate unit price application are required. We also consider nearby noise complaints when planning.
After construction completion, we support commissioning stages for site operation such as pneumatic or hydraulic tests, flow tests, and valve opening/closing commissioning in the presence of customers.
Yes. After construction completion, we provide delivery document files including construction completion reports, installation photos, material lists, test certificates, performance confirmations, and mechanical equipment maintenance manuals. For public institutions, format conversion according to regulation forms is also possible.
For all pipe construction, we mandatorily conduct soap bubble leak tests and pneumatic/hydraulic leak tests, and results are provided as test records and construction photos. Tests can also be conducted in the presence of customers or third-party supervision.
Even after the warranty period (normally 1 year from construction completion) ends, paid maintenance contracts or individual A/S are possible. When failures are reported, we operate a system of dispatching within 48 hours and emergency dispatch on the same day when necessary (centered on metropolitan/Honam areas), and parts replacement is processed directly from our dedicated inventory center.
